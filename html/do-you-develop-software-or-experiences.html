Do You Develop Software or Experiences?

Sun 20 December 2009
By Kenneth Reitz

In Code.

I read an interesting article today on Apple's marketing strategy. A certain section stood out to me, regarding their hardware manufacturing: > Apple is an experience company. They're a high-end marque; if they > were in the automobile business, they'd be BMW, Mercedes, and > Porsche rolled into one. They own about 12% of the PC market in the > USA... but 91% of the high end of the PC market (laptops over $999, > desktops over $699).

— Charlie Stross of Antipope.org.

## The Point: Seth Godin and 37Signals both recommend marketing yourself to the early adopters and geeks. Apple, however, does the opposite. They market themselves to the middle market — the incredibly non-technical. The geeks come on their own, with no marketing needed. They love it. And this makes Apple *enormously* successful. **Develop your software/experience for the masses**. Make the geeks love it, but make them find it on their own. Just a thought.
